# Muharraq-Style Traditional Neighborhood Kit — Production Specification

## Purpose

Fictionalized traditional neighborhood system inspired by Bahrain’s compact pearling-era urban fabric without reproducing protected buildings.

## Modules and dimensions

- party-wall bay: 2.5 or 3.0 m W × 3.0 m H
- timber door bay: 1.2 × 2.3 m opening
- projecting window bay: 1.5 m opening, 0.25 m projection
- alley arch: 2.5–3.5 m span, 3.2 m clearance
- parapet: 0.6–0.9 m
- courtyard hint module: 4 × 4 m non-enterable visual recess
- shop bay: 2.5 m shutter/door module
- roof utility group: tank, AC screen, vent and restrained cable anchors
- shade canopy sockets at 2.8–3.2 m height

## Visual rules

Warm white/beige plaster, coral-stone-inspired texture language, dark wood doors, geometric vents, deep shadow, small openings and restrained restoration/weathering. Utility wiring is simplified and safe-looking; no extreme decay.

## Budget

- façade set per building: 2,500–7,500 triangles LOD0
- LOD1: 35–45%
- distant connected block: 500–1,200 triangles per building footprint
- materials: shared plaster/stone/wood trim atlas + optional glass
- texture: 1024 trim atlas, 512 decals

## Collision

Merged boxes per connected block, doorway triggers separately. Decorative vents, cables, lamps and AC units have no collision.
