# Bahrain Brick Art Style Bible

## Identity

Bahrain Brick is a premium stylized low-poly Android open world: urban, coastal, multicultural, modern and rooted in Bahraini architectural logic. It is not a grim crime-game imitation, a generic Gulf megacity, or a desert-fantasy theme park.

## Silhouette hierarchy

- Hero skyline: a small number of clean, distinctive but fictional tower silhouettes.
- Midground: broad podiums, shaded balconies, parapets, canopies, arcades and boundary walls.
- Street level: large readable shop bays, recessed entrances, awnings, railings, AC units, palms and signage panels.
- Micro-detail: texture/normal/decals, not geometry, unless interactive.

## Shape language

- Strong horizontal roof lines and shaded recesses.
- Modular wall bays in 2 m, 3 m and 4 m widths.
- Repeated windows using atlases or trim sheets.
- Rounded kerbs and traffic islands.
- Geometric screens and simplified wooden lattice details.
- Domes and minarets only where contextually appropriate.
- Waterfront elements use curves, terraces and horizontal railings.

## Palette

Core: warm white, ivory, sand, pale limestone, beige plaster, muted terracotta, charcoal asphalt, dark reflective glass, turquoise water, dusty green planting, warm wood and brushed metal.

Accents: restrained Bahrain-inspired red/white, teal, yellow and blue. Commercial neon is localized and low-intensity.

## Surface treatment

- Base colors remain readable under midday sun.
- Roughness separation communicates material more than complex reflections.
- Dust/salt/sun bleaching are overlays, not full-object grime.
- Traditional districts may be weathered but not dominated by neglect.
- No photogrammetry or high-frequency scan noise.

## Mobile readability gates

Interactive items must read at 5 m and 15 m on a 6-inch screen at medium settings. Minimum icon silhouette thickness is 3 px at target render size. Important doorways, mission props and vehicle lights must retain value contrast under midday and dusk presets.

## Cultural controls

- Architecture is inspired by observable regional typologies, not copied landmark geometry.
- Arabic text requires verified spelling and purpose.
- Sacred text is excluded from decorative generation.
- Mosques are respectful civic/spiritual spaces, not generic combat arenas.
- Clothing and population diversity are represented without caricature.
- Businesses and liveries are fictional.
